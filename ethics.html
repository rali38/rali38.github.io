IT-Related Ethics

Ethics in information technology focuses on using technology responsibly, protecting users, and reducing harm.

Why Ethics Matters in IT

Technology affects how people communicate, work, learn, and store personal information. Because of this, IT professionals have a responsibility to make decisions that protect users and support the public good. The ACM Code of Ethics explains that computing professionals should contribute to society, avoid harm, be honest, respect privacy, and act responsibly when using computing technology (Association for Computing Machinery, 2018).

Guidelines for Developers

Developers should create software that is safe, reliable, and respectful of users. The IEEE/ACM Software Engineering Code of Ethics explains that software engineers should act consistently with the public interest and maintain integrity in their professional work (IEEE Computer Society & Association for Computing Machinery, n.d.).

  • Write secure and reliable code.
  • Be honest about what software can and cannot do.
  • Protect private and sensitive user information.
  • Test software carefully before releasing it.
  • Give credit when using other people’s work or ideas.

Ethical Considerations for Data Scientists

Data scientists must be careful when collecting, analyzing, and using data. Ethical data work requires privacy, fairness, transparency, and accuracy. Data should not be used in ways that mislead people, expose private information, or create unfair outcomes.

  • Collect only the data that is necessary.
  • Protect data from unauthorized access.
  • Check data for bias and unfair patterns.
  • Explain how data is being used when possible.
  • Avoid using data in ways that could harm individuals or groups.

Responsible Artificial Intelligence

Artificial intelligence systems can affect decisions in areas such as hiring, education, security, healthcare, and finance. The National Institute of Standards and Technology explains that trustworthy AI should be valid, reliable, safe, secure, accountable, transparent, explainable, privacy-enhanced, and fair with harmful bias managed (NIST, 2023).

  • Test AI systems before using them.
  • Monitor AI systems after release.
  • Reduce bias in AI training data and results.
  • Make AI decisions explainable when possible.
  • Keep human review involved in important decisions.
